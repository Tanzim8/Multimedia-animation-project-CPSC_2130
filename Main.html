In the Quiet Flow of Water Lies a Poem

“All the water below me came from above … the sea remembers and sings back from the depth where nothing is forgotten.”

Waves

The rolling, rhythmic rise and fall of waves evokes the breath of the earth — a constant, fluid dialogue between sea and sky.

River

Rivers carve their way through landscapes as life carves meaning through the soul. They whisper about journey, transformation, and persistence.

Rain

Gentle or stormy, rain cleanses. It is a soft return, a gift returning from the sky, bringing renewal in every drop.

Still Water

When water lies still, it reflects. In its calmness, of the world and of ourselves, we find clarity and silent truth.

About This Poetry

Like the poet David Whyte, this site draws inspiration from the deep metaphors of nature. His work often weaves together themes of belonging, loss, and the elemental beauty of life.

Here, water is more than physical: it is symbolism. The ocean, the rain, the rivers—they are invitations to reflect on our inner currents and the beauty in flowing forward.
